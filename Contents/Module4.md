# Exercise 1 – Automated Shopping Bill

## Problem Statement

A grocery store wants to prepare an automated billing system.

Each customer purchases multiple items, and the bill should automatically calculate:

- Total cost of each item
- Grand Total
- GST
- Final Payable Amount

### 📥 Practice File

➡️ **[Shopping_Bill_Template.xlsx](Templates/Shopping_Bill_Template.xlsx)**

---

## Given Data

| Item | Quantity | Rate (₹/kg) |
|------|---------:|------------:|
| Rice | 5 | 65 |
| Sugar | 2 | 48 |
| Wheat Flour | 10 | 38 |
| Pulses | 3 | 110 |
| Cooking Oil | 2 | 170 |

---

## Students Will Calculate

- Item Cost = Quantity × Rate
- Grand Total
- GST @ 5%
- Final Payable Amount

---

## Excel Concepts Used

- Multiplication
- SUM()
- Percentage Calculation
- Relative Cell References
- Currency Formatting
- AutoFill

---

## Expected Output

Students should create a professional billing worksheet displaying:

- Item-wise Cost
- Grand Total
- GST
- Final Bill Amount

# Exercise 2 – Paint Requirement Calculator

## Problem Statement

A contractor wishes to estimate the amount of paint required for a room.

Students will prepare an Excel worksheet to calculate:

- Total wall area
- Area of doors
- Area of windows
- Net paintable area
- Quantity of paint required
- Estimated painting cost

### 📥 Practice File

➡️ **[Paint_Estimation_Template.xlsx](Templates/Paint_Estimation_Template.xlsx)**

---

## Given Data

### Room Dimensions

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|------:|
| Length | 5 m |
| Width | 4 m |
| Height | 3 m |

### Openings

| Opening | Dimensions |
|----------|------------|
| Door | 2 m × 1 m |
| Window 1 | 1.5 m × 1.2 m |
| Window 2 | 1.5 m × 1.2 m |

Additional Information

- Paint Coverage = **10 m²/Litre**
- Paint Cost = **₹350/Litre**

---

## Students Will Calculate

1. Total Wall Area

2. Total Area of Doors & Windows

3. Net Paintable Area

4. Paint Required

5. Estimated Painting Cost

---

## Excel Concepts Used

- Formula Creation
- Multiplication
- Addition
- Subtraction
- Division
- ROUND()
- Cell Referencing

---

## Expected Output

Students should prepare a professional worksheet displaying:

- Input Data
- Calculated Areas
- Paint Quantity Required
- Estimated Cost

# 💡 Best Practices

- Use meaningful headings.
- Avoid typing numbers directly inside formulas.
- Always use cell references.
- Check formulas before copying.
- Maintain consistent units.
- Save your work regularly.
- Keep worksheets neat and organized.
- Prepare print-friendly worksheets.
